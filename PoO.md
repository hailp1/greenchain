# Research Framework: Marketing & Consumer Behavior (S-O-R Focus)

**Title:** Beyond Traceability: How Blockchain-Verified Origin Signals Influence Consumer Trust and Purchase Intention in High-Involvement Agricultural Products

**Theoretical Framework:** Stimulus-Organism-Response (S-O-R) & Signaling Theory

---

### 1. Conceptual Model (Mô hình nghiên cứu)

In this research, we position the **ST-PoO Protocol** as the primary **Stimulus**.

*   **Stimulus (S):** Blockchain-Verified Origin signals (Real-time IoT data, immutable geo-fencing, and decentralized social consensus).
*   **Organism (O):** Perceived Transparency, Perceived Quality, and Brand Trust.
*   **Response (R):** Purchase Intention and Willingness to Pay a Premium (WTP).

#### Research Hypotheses (Giả thuyết nghiên cứu):
*   **H1:** High-fidelity blockchain signals (ST-PoO) have a positive impact on perceived transparency.
*   **H2:** Perceived transparency positively mediates the relationship between blockchain signals and brand trust.
*   **H3:** Brand trust significantly influences the consumer's willingness to pay a premium for agricultural products.

---

### 2. The Marketing Logic: Signaling Theory

According to **Signaling Theory**, in markets with high information asymmetry (like organic agriculture), consumers look for signals to assess unobservable quality.
*   **Traditional Signals:** Paper certifications, brand names (Low reliability, high forgery risk).
*   **ST-PoO as a "Costly Signal":** Because the ST-PoO protocol requires real-time IoT verification and social consensus, it is difficult and costly to fake. Therefore, it serves as a "Superior Signal" that effectively reduces consumer perceived risk.

---

### 3. Methodology: Experimental Design

To prove this in a Marketing journal, we use a **2x2 Between-Subjects Experimental Design**:

1.  **Group A (Control):** Traditional QR code (linking to a static web page).
2.  **Group B (Experimental):** Blockchain-verified QR code (linking to our **fwd LIFEchain Explorer** with ST-PoO data).

**Measurement:** We use a 7-point Likert scale to measure:
*   *Trust:* "I believe this product truly comes from the stated origin."
*   *Purchase Intention:* "I am likely to buy this product over a cheaper alternative."

---

### 4. Mathematical Modeling in Marketing

In marketing journals, we often use **Structural Equation Modeling (SEM)**. The "thickness" of the paper will come from the **Path Coefficients**:

$$Trust = \beta_1 \cdot Transparency + \beta_2 \cdot Blockchain\_Signal + \epsilon$$

We will use the **fwd LIFEchain** platform to collect real user interactions and feed them into the model to prove that users who interact with the Explorer have a significantly higher Trust score than those who don't.

---

### 5. Why this is a Q2 Marketing Paper?

*   **Novel Stimulus:** Most marketing papers talk about "Online Reviews" or "Influencers". Using a **Blockchain Protocol** as a psychological stimulus is a cutting-edge "Mar-Tech" (Marketing Technology) topic.
*   **Solution to Green-washing:** The paper provides a technical solution to "Green-washing" (the act of making false claims about environmental efforts), which is a hot topic in *Sustainability* and *Marketing* journals.
*   **Managerial Implications:** The paper will advise agricultural managers on how to use blockchain as a "Trust-building tool" rather than just a "Logistics tool".
